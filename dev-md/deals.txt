# Offer & Deal Architecture

## Overview

Offer & Deal is the business collaboration system of Contact.

It provides a unified workflow for proposing, accepting, managing and completing business collaborations between two or more participants.

Unlike traditional CRM or project management software, Contact treats every collaboration as a simple lifecycle:

────────────────────────────────
Offer Creation
├── Custom Offer (Message)
├── AI Offer Draft (Message)
└── Card Offer Template
          │
          ▼
        Offer
          │
          ▼
      Accepted
          │
          ▼
         Deal
          │
          ▼
     Completed
          │
          ▼
      Deal Again
          │
          ▼
     New Draft Offer
────────────────────────────────

Every collaboration always begins with an Offer.
A Deal only exists after an Offer has been accepted.
Once accepted, all future collaboration is managed inside the Deal.

This keeps communication, meetings, files and payments organized around a single collaboration instead of scattering them across multiple products.

---

# Philosophy

The philosophy of Contact is:
> Communication creates Offers.
> Offers create Deals.
> Deals create Value.

Contact is designed to automate repetitive business communication while allowing users to remain in complete control of every important business decision.

As an AI-native contact platform, Contact gradually assists users by:
- AI answers client questions.
- AI understands client requirements.
- AI suggests solutions.
- AI generates Offer drafts.
- AI assists negotiations.
- AI guides the payment process.

However:
- AI never sends Offers automatically.
- AI never accepts Offers automatically.
- AI never completes Deals automatically.

Every Offer must always be reviewed and approved by the user before being sent.
The user always owns the final decision.

Contact never participates in payments.
Contact never processes payments.
Contact never collects platform fees.
Contact never calculates taxes.
Contact only records the commercial agreement between two parties.

---

## Offer Entry Points

Offers can be created from three entry points.

### 1. Custom Offer

Users can create a Custom Offer from any conversation, including:
- Messages
- Live Chat

Users may also start a conversation from the Contacts page before creating an Offer.

Selecting **Send Offer** opens the Offer editor.

After the Offer is sent, it appears as an interactive Offer Card inside the conversation.

---

### 2. AI Offer Draft

AI can generate an Offer draft based on the current conversation.

AI suggests:

- Title
- Description
- Amount
- Discount
- Delivery
- Expiration

Users must review, edit and approve the Offer before sending.

AI can never send an Offer automatically.

After approval, the Offer is sent as an interactive Offer Card inside the conversation.

---

### 3. Card Offer Template

Offer Templates can be used in two ways.

#### Public Contact Card

- Visitors can open the user's public Contact Card.
- Visitors can select an Offer Template directly from the Offer section.
- Selecting an Offer Template immediately creates a new Offer instance for that client.

#### Shared Offer Template Link

- Users can share a direct link to any Offer Template inside a conversation.
- Recipients can open the Offer Template directly without browsing the Contact Card.
- Selecting the shared Offer Template immediately creates a new Offer instance.

---

# Core Concepts

Offer and Deal are different concepts.

## Offer

An Offer is a business proposal.

An Offer is negotiable until accepted.

Offers are lightweight.

Offers do not manage collaboration.

Offers do not manage payments.

Offers do not manage meetings.

Offers do not manage files.

---

## Deal

A Deal represents an accepted Offer.

A Deal becomes the collaboration workspace.

Every Deal contains the collaboration history, including:

- Timeline
- Messages
- Meetings
- Files
- Payments
- Completion

Deals represent active or completed business relationships.

## Offer Template

An Offer Template is reusable.

Examples:

- Monthly Consulting
- Patent Application
- European Patent Application

Offer Templates are published on the user's Contact Card.

Offer Templates never become Deals directly.

Selecting an Offer Template always creates a new Offer.

---

## Offer

An Offer is an independent proposal sent to a client.

Every Offer is unique.

Even when created from the same template, every Offer becomes an independent Offer instance.

Each Offer maintains its own:

- Title
- Description
- Amount
- Participants
- Created At
- Status

Offers are immutable after acceptance.

---

## Deal

A Deal is created only after an Offer has been accepted.

Deals are never created directly.

Every Deal originates from exactly one accepted Offer.

A Deal represents the collaboration itself rather than the proposal.

# Design Principles

Offer & Deal follows several core principles.

## Single Collaboration

Every collaboration has exactly one active Deal.

---

## One Offer → One Deal

One accepted Offer creates exactly one Deal.

An Offer can never create multiple Deals.

---

## Deals Never Merge

Deals always remain independent.

Even if the same participants collaborate multiple times, each collaboration becomes a separate Deal.

---

## History Never Changes

Accepted Offers never change.

Completed Deals never change.

Historical business records are preserved permanently.

---

## Collaboration Is Continuous

Completing a Deal does not end the relationship.

Future collaborations should continue through Deal Again, which creates a new Offer and eventually a new Deal.

This keeps every business transaction independent while preserving the complete relationship history.


## Deals List

- Deals are displayed as a table.
- Each row represents one Deal.
- Each Deal occupies one row.
- Rows have a fixed height.
- Columns remain aligned across all rows.
- The list is optimized for scanning large numbers of Deals.

## Deals Tabs

Tabs:
- All (16)
- Open (5)
- Completed (11)
- Canceled
The selected tab filters the Deal list.
The count displayed beside each tab updates automatically.

## Deal Cover

- Every Deal displays a cover image 100x100px.
- Template Deals use the corresponding Offer Template cover.
- Custom Deals use the default Custom Offer cover.
- Cover size is fixed.
- Cover is not clickable.
- Cover is used only for visual identification.


## Deal Title

- Every Deal displays:
  - Title
  - Order ID
- Title is displayed above the Order ID.
- Order ID is displayed below the Title.
- Order ID includes a Copy button.
Example:

- Clicking Copy copies the Order ID to the clipboard.
- Order ID is never editable.


## Amount

Amount displays the final accepted Deal amount.
Amount is immutable after the Offer is accepted.
Amount always uses the Deal currency.


## Status

Supported statuses:
- Open
- Completed
- Canceled

Status is system controlled.
Users cannot edit Deal status directly from the Deal List.


## Sorting

The following columns support sorting:
- Order Date
- Due Date
- Amount
- Status

Sorting behavior:
- First click:
  Ascending
- Second click:
  Descending
- Only one active sorting column is allowed.
- The active sorting column displays the sorting direction.
- Default sorting:
Order Date (Descending)
Newest Deals appear first.


## Search

- Search filters the current Deal list.
- Search matches:
  - Title
  - Client
  - Order ID
- Search updates results in real time.
- Search only affects the currently selected tab.

## Provider ↔ Client

The Deal List uses a single relationship column.
The displayed column name changes based on the current user's role in the Deal.

### When the current user is the Provider:
Column name: Client
Display: Client display name
Selecting the Client opens the client's public Contact Card.

### When the current user is the Client:
Column name: Provider
Display: Provider display name
Selecting the Provider opens the provider's public Contact Card.

A Deal always contains two parties:
- Provider: the user who creates and provides the Offer.
- Client: the user who accepts the Offer.

  The original Deal remains unchanged.
- Archive moves the Deal into Archived Deals.

### Deal Again
Deal Again allows the client to create a new Offer based on a previously completed Deal.
Deal Again is only available when all of the following conditions are met:
- Deal status is Completed.
- The provider account still exists.
- The provider's Offer feature is enabled.
- The provider's required verification remains valid.

If any condition is not satisfied:
- Deal Again is hidden.

Deal Again never modifies the existing Deal.
Selecting Deal Again starts a new Offer flow with the same provider.
The original Deal remains unchanged.

### Row Menu for Provider
Each Deal provides a context menu.
Menu:

Offer Again
──────────
Archive

- Open opens the Deal Detail page.
- Offer Again creates a new Offer based on the previous Deal.
  The original Deal remains unchanged.
- Archive moves the Deal into Archived Deals.
  Archive never deletes a Deal.

# Offer

## Overview

An Offer is a business proposal.

An Offer represents a client's opportunity to accept or reject a proposed collaboration.

Offers are intentionally lightweight.

They describe:

- What will be delivered
- How much it costs

They do not manage the collaboration itself.

Once accepted, collaboration moves into a Deal.

- another Offer
- the original Offer Template

## Rejected

Client rejected the Offer.

Rejected Offers become read-only.

Rejected Offers never create Deals.

---

## Expired

Offer expired before client acceptance.

Expired Offers become read-only.

Expired Offers never create Deals.

Rejected Offers cannot be edited.

Expired Offers cannot be edited.

# Offer Template

Offer Templates exist to eliminate repetitive work.

Examples:

- Monthly Consulting
- Patent application

Offer Templates contain reusable defaults.

## Offer Template URL

Generate a public URL for every Offer Template.

Format:
card.contact/{username}/offers/{offer-slug}

Example:
card.contact/john/offers/european-patent-application

Offer slugs are generated automatically from the Offer Title.

Slug generation rules:
- Convert all characters to lowercase.
- Replace spaces with hyphens (-).
- Remove unsupported URL characters.
- Consecutive hyphens should be collapsed into a single hyphen.
- Leading and trailing hyphens should be removed.

Offer slugs must be unique within the same user's Offer Templates.
If a slug already exists, append an incremental suffix automatically.

Example:
website-redesign
european-patent-application

Editing the Offer Title automatically updates the Offer slug.

Previous public URLs should automatically redirect to the latest Offer URL to preserve existing shared links and search engine indexing.

## Delivery

- Every Offer includes an Due Date time.
- Delivery represents the expected completion period after the Offer is accepted.
- Delivery does not start when the Offer is created.
- Delivery starts only after the client accepts the Offer and the Deal is created.

- Users can choose:
  - 3 Days
  - 7 Days
  - 14 Days
  - 30 Days
  - 60 Days
  - 90 Days
  - Specific Date

- Delivery can be defined as:
  - A duration in days.
  - A specific calendar date.

- When a duration is selected:
  - The system automatically calculates the Due Date date based on the Offer acceptance date.

- When Specific Date is selected:
  - The user selects the Due Date date manually.

- Delivery information is transferred to the Deal after the Offer is accepted.


## Amount
- Every Offer includes an amount.
- Amount consists of:
  - Currency
  - Value

## Final Amount

- The Final Amount is calculated automatically.

Formula:

Final Amount = Amount − Discount

- Final Amount updates immediately whenever:
  - Amount changes.
  - Discount type changes.
  - Discount value changes.

- Final Amount cannot be negative.

## Reviews & Reputation

Completed Deals support different reputation mechanisms depending on the Offer type.


### Offer Template Deals

Offer Template Deals support both:
- Rating
- Optional written review

Ratings and reviews are publicly associated with the corresponding Offer Template.
Their purpose is to help future clients evaluate that specific Offer Template.
Offer Template ratings never affect the provider's overall public reputation.

Removing an Offer Template also removes its associated public ratings and reviews.


### Custom Offer Deals

Custom Offer Deals also support:
- Rating
- Optional written review

Custom Offer ratings and reviews are private.
Only the provider can view feedback received from completed Custom Offers.
Their purpose is to help providers improve future collaborations.

Custom Offer ratings and reviews are never displayed publicly and never affect the provider's public profile.


### Completed Collaborations

Every successfully completed Deal contributes to the provider's Completed Collaborations count.

This includes:
- Offer Template Deals
- Custom Offer Deals

Completed Collaborations represent overall business experience rather than public ratings.


### Review Flow

When a Deal is completed, clients are invited to leave:
- A rating
- An optional written review

After submitting the review, clients may also choose to award an Honor to the provider.

Awarding an Honor is optional and independent of the review.


# Deal

## Overview

A Deal represents an accepted Offer.

A Deal is the collaboration workspace where both parties communicate, complete work, exchange files, receive payments and finish the collaboration.

Unlike an Offer, a Deal is no longer a proposal.

A Deal represents an active business relationship.

    │

    ▼
Deal
────────────────────────────────

Every Deal originates from exactly one accepted Offer.

Deals are never created manually.

Deals are never created directly.
